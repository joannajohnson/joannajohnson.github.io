Synthesis

As I was growing up, my mother would read to me and my sisters on our back porch at night. It was books like Green Eggs and Ham and Little House on the Prairie that made me want to go into book publishing. Whether it was the rhymes and comic nature or the sense of adventure, I became hooked on these books and others that followed. I came to realize the importance of books and their ability to widen one’s mind. I wanted to be a part of creating inspiring books like the ones from my childhood, which I still enjoy today. As a middle-aged kid, I always enjoyed writing fiction stories, but it wasn’t until high school that I took a creative writing class. I experimented with novel writing then and soon came to crave NaNoWriMo (the National Novel Writing Month). Later, I was accepted into Virginia Tech as an early decision applicant and knew I wanted to pursue an English major. I put myself on the Creative Writing and Professional Writing tracks, which was the smartest decision of my years as an undergrad. The Professional Writing focus required me to enroll in classes such as Designing Documents for Print, Developing Online Content, Technical Editing and Style, Science Writing, and Feminism and Interaction Design. These specific classes greatly helped shape the way I think. They also taught me additional skills thought to be outside the scope of English such as design and coding (HTML, CSS, Java). Importantly, they also better introduced and informed me on another passion: editing. The power of words when rearranged a certain way or used effectively is awe-inspiring, not to mention the power of correct punctuation and grammar. It is for this reason that I aim to acquire a job in the editorial department of a publishing company. I know I can help produce books that hold as much impact and pleasure as the aforementioned books did for me. Every story just needs a little help and a lot of minds to put it on its way.
